The honest truth

Does a degree actually
matter?

For DevOps Engineer roles in 2026 — less than most people think. Companies care whether you can do the job. A portfolio of real work, demonstrated skills, and domain expertise from your previous career are the credentials that actually move hiring decisions.

What actually gets you hired

Hiring managers evaluate your ability to do the work — through portfolio, case studies, certifications, or technical assessments. None of these require a university degree. The credential that matters is demonstrated competence.

Where degree matters less

Most companies

Startups, scale-ups, and most technology companies hire devops engineers on demonstrated skills. Your ability to show your work matters more than where you studied.

Where degree matters more

Some large enterprises

Certain large corporates and regulated industries retain degree requirements for compliance reasons. These are a declining fraction of available roles.

Myth vs reality

What people get
wrong.

Myth
You need a traditional degree to be taken seriously as a devops engineer.
Reality
Hiring managers evaluate your portfolio, case studies, and interview performance — not your degree. Demonstrated ability consistently outweighs credentials.
Myth
Self-taught professionals can't reach senior devops engineer roles.
Reality
Seniority is determined by your ability to solve hard problems, mentor others, and deliver results — not your educational background.
Myth
Certifications can't replace a degree.
Reality
In many technical fields, certifications demonstrate specific, current competency more effectively than a degree. Employers increasingly recognise this.
Myth
Without a degree you'll always earn less.
Reality
Compensation is driven by the value you deliver, your negotiation, and the company — not your degree. Skilled professionals without degrees regularly earn more than degree holders in the same roles.

The path

How to get hired
without a degree.

1
Month 1–3

Build the foundations

Focus on the core skills required for devops engineer roles. Use free and low-cost resources — documentation, YouTube, community courses. The goal is enough foundation to start building real projects.

2
Month 2–5

Build something real

Apply what you're learning immediately. Build a project in your previous industry domain — your domain knowledge makes it more valuable than a generic project. Deploy it. Make it real.

3
Month 4–8

Build your portfolio

Document your work clearly — the problem, your approach, your decisions, and what you'd do differently. This portfolio replaces your degree in the hiring process. Quality over quantity.

4
Month 6–12

Get certifications if relevant

In some fields, certifications pass HR filters that might otherwise screen you. Research which certifications are respected in devops engineer hiring specifically and pursue the most relevant one.

5
Month 8–18

Target the right companies

Target companies where humans review applications — startups and scale-ups rather than large enterprises with automated screening. Your domain expertise from a previous career is a genuine differentiator.
