The honest truth

Does a degree actually
matter here?

Solutions Architecture is fundamentally an experience-based role. The ability to design systems that are scalable, reliable, secure, and cost-effective comes from having built and operated real systems — seen what breaks under load, what creates security vulnerabilities, what costs spiral out of control, and what trade-offs matter in practice. A CS degree teaches theory; architecture experience teaches judgment.

Cloud providers have institutionalised this with their certification programmes. AWS Solutions Architect Professional, Google Cloud Professional Cloud Architect, and Microsoft Azure Solutions Architect Expert are rigorous, scenario-based exams that test exactly the kind of architectural judgment that hiring managers need to evaluate. These certifications carry genuine weight — often more than a degree — in solutions architecture hiring.

What actually gets you hired

AWS Solutions Architect Professional certification plus the ability to discuss real architectural decisions you've made — why you chose one approach over another, what the trade-offs were, what you'd do differently now. Hiring managers assess judgment and communication as much as technical knowledge. The certification proves the knowledge; the conversation proves the judgment.

Where degree matters less

Cloud consultancies and tech companies

Companies like Slalom, Accenture's cloud practice, Thoughtworks, and most tech companies evaluate solutions architects on certification level, architectural design ability, and the projects they can discuss. Self-taught engineers with strong cloud certification stacks are hired consistently.

Where degree matters more

Government and defence contracts

Some government and defence architecture roles have formal education requirements tied to contract compliance. These roles typically pay less and move slower than commercial architecture roles. Commercial cloud architecture is a more accessible and faster-growing market.

Myth vs reality

What people get
wrong.

Myth
Solutions Architect is just a senior developer title.
Reality
Solutions Architects design systems but typically don't write production code day to day. The role is about making design decisions — choosing between microservices and monolith, deciding on database architecture, designing for failure, optimising for cost — and communicating those decisions to both technical teams and business stakeholders. It's a distinct role, not just a senior engineering title.
Myth
You need to have been a software engineer to become a solutions architect.
Reality
Software engineering is the most common background, but cloud engineers, DevOps engineers, and experienced sysadmins also transition successfully. What matters is deep experience with how systems behave in production — failures, scaling challenges, security incidents, cost surprises. That experience comes from operations and infrastructure backgrounds as much as from development.
Myth
You need to know every AWS service to become an architect.
Reality
You need to know the core services deeply — compute, storage, networking, databases, security, and identity — and understand the principles well enough to evaluate unfamiliar services quickly. The AWS Solutions Architect exams test this pattern: given a business requirement, choose the right service and configuration. It's about applying principles, not memorising the entire AWS catalogue.
Myth
Solutions Architecture is a role you grow into, not one you transition to.
Reality
Many Solutions Architects do grow into the role from engineering. But career changers from cloud engineering, DevOps, and technical sales (Solutions Engineer → Solutions Architect) also make successful transitions. The key is accumulating the architectural experience and certification credentials, not following a specific linear path.

The path

How to get hired
without a degree.

1
Month 1–4

Earn AWS Solutions Architect Associate first

If you don't already have it, AWS SAA-C03 is the foundation. Study with Adrian Cantrill's course — it's the most thorough available and teaches architectural reasoning, not just service knowledge. Use TutorialsDojo practice exams to assess readiness. The Associate exam is a prerequisite for the Professional — don't skip straight to Professional without a solid Associate foundation.

2
2
Month 4–9

Study the AWS Well-Architected Framework deeply

The five pillars — Operational Excellence, Security, Reliability, Performance Efficiency, and Cost Optimisation — are the framework through which professional architects evaluate every design decision. Read the full framework documentation. Work through the Well-Architected Tool on real or simulated workloads. This framework appears extensively in Professional exam scenarios and in real architectural conversations with clients.

3
Month 6–12

Earn AWS Solutions Architect Professional

This is the credential that moves hiring decisions in solutions architecture. The Professional exam is significantly harder than the Associate — multi-service scenarios, cost optimisation trade-offs, migration strategies, and disaster recovery design. Cantrill's SAP-C02 course is again the best preparation resource. Many candidates need 6 months of focused study after their Associate. Don't rush it.

4
Month 8–14

Document your architectural decisions formally

Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) are how professional architects document why they made the choices they made — what alternatives were considered, what trade-offs were accepted, and what the implications are. Start writing ADRs for any architectural decisions you make in your current role or in personal projects. A portfolio of real ADRs demonstrates architectural thinking in a way that no certification can.

5
Month 12–20

Target cloud consultancies and architect roles at tech companies

Cloud consultancies (Slalom, Wipro, Rackspace, smaller AWS Premier Partners) are the most reliable entry point for self-taught solutions architects. They provide exposure to many different client environments, accelerating your experience accumulation faster than any single employer. AWS also employs Solutions Architects who work with customers — their hiring process is certification-focused and explicitly values non-traditional backgrounds.

FAQ

Common questions.

Solutions Architects design the technical architecture for specific solutions or projects — choosing services, designing integrations, ensuring scalability. Cloud Architects focus specifically on cloud infrastructure design. Enterprise Architects work at the organisational level — standardising technology patterns, managing technical debt, and aligning technology to business strategy across the whole organisation. Enterprise Architecture is a distinct and more senior discipline. Start with Solutions Architecture.
AWS has the largest market share and the most solutions architect job listings — AWS SAP (Solutions Architect Professional) is the highest-value single certification for this role. Azure's Solutions Architect Expert is the equivalent for Microsoft-stack environments. GCP's Professional Cloud Architect is relevant at Google Cloud-focused companies. Start with AWS unless you're specifically targeting Azure or GCP environments. The architectural principles transfer between platforms.
Most solutions architect roles expect 5–8 years of relevant technical experience. This isn't a first career step — it's typically where cloud engineers, DevOps engineers, and senior developers with strong cloud skills progress to. If you're earlier in your journey, target cloud engineering or senior engineering roles first and build toward architecture. The AWS Professional certification combined with 4–5 years of hands-on cloud experience is a realistic benchmark for a first architect application.
Solutions Architect at a tech company or consultancy: $140k–$190k. Senior Solutions Architect: $180k–$240k. Principal or Distinguished Architect: $230k–$320k+. AWS employs Solutions Architects who work directly with enterprise customers — their salaries are competitive with the market and the role provides exceptional breadth of architectural exposure. Solutions architecture is one of the highest-compensated non-management technical roles available.
Exceptionally so. Architecture is one of the few senior technical roles where the work remains primarily technical — you're not forced into management to progress. The career tracks to Principal Architect and Distinguished Architect at large companies, or to CTO at smaller ones. The skills are also durable — architectural thinking and the ability to design reliable, scalable systems remain valuable regardless of which specific cloud services or programming languages are in fashion.
