The honest truth

Does a degree actually
matter here?

AI Consulting is a trust business. Clients hire people they believe can navigate both the technology and their industry's specific constraints. A finance professional who understands AI is more valuable to a bank than a computer scientist who has never seen a trading desk. A healthcare operations manager who understands LLMs is more valuable to a hospital system than an ML researcher who has never dealt with clinical workflows.

The degree that matters in consulting is the one from your previous career — whatever domain knowledge you've spent years accumulating. That's what clients are actually paying for.

What actually gets you hired or retained

A clearly defined specialisation. Not "AI consultant" — "AI adoption consultant for financial services firms under $500M AUM" or "AI workflow consultant for legal teams." The narrower your positioning, the higher your rates and the shorter your sales cycle. Clients don't hire generalists when they can find a specialist.

Where degree matters less

Independent consulting and boutiques

Clients hiring directly care about your track record, referrals, and whether you understand their business. Nobody asks for a transcript. Your portfolio of past engagements is the credential that matters.

Where degree matters more

Large consultancy hiring processes

Deloitte, McKinsey, and Accenture often have degree requirements in HR screening for junior hires. Senior lateral hires with strong track records are evaluated differently — but the entry path at large firms is more credential-dependent.

Myth vs reality

What people get
wrong.

Myth
You need deep technical AI skills to consult on AI.
Reality
You need enough AI literacy to evaluate solutions, spot vendor hype, and have credible conversations with technical teams. The business judgment — knowing what problems are worth solving and whether AI is actually the right tool — is rarer and more valuable.
Myth
The market is too crowded with AI consultants already.
Reality
The market is crowded with people who attended a two-day AI workshop and call themselves consultants. It is severely short of people who combine genuine domain expertise with real AI implementation experience. Those people command rates of $250–$600/hour.
Myth
You need to build AI tools to consult on them.
Reality
You need to understand them well enough to evaluate them, pilot them, and guide adoption. Many of the most effective AI consultants have never written a line of Python. They know how to run a structured pilot, measure results, and manage the organisational change AI requires.
Myth
AI consulting is a short-term trend that will dry up.
Reality
Most organisations are 3–5 years into what will be a decade-long AI adoption journey. The need for trusted advisors who can navigate this — across strategy, vendor selection, change management, and governance — is structural, not cyclical.

The path

How to get hired
without a degree.

1
Month 1–3

Build genuine AI literacy in your domain

Don't learn AI generally — learn how AI is being applied specifically in your previous industry. If you're from healthcare, understand how LLMs are being used in clinical documentation, diagnostic support, and patient communication. Read industry-specific AI case studies. The goal is to speak about AI applications in your domain with enough depth that clients trust your judgment.

2
Month 2–5

Define your specific positioning

The fastest path to consulting revenue is a narrow, specific positioning. "I help mid-sized law firms evaluate and implement AI for document review and contract analysis" is sellable. "AI consultant" is not. Your positioning should combine your domain expertise with a specific AI use case or client segment. Write it down and test it with people in your network.

3
Month 3–6

Build your advisory framework

Clients pay for structured thinking, not just information. Develop your approach to AI opportunity assessment — how do you identify where AI creates value in an organisation? How do you evaluate vendors? How do you design a 90-day pilot? These frameworks are your intellectual property and what differentiates you from someone who just attended an AI conference.

4
Month 4–8

Get your first engagement — any way you can

Offer pro bono advisory to a company in your network. Volunteer to lead an AI evaluation project at your current employer. Consulting requires a track record, and the first engagement is always the hardest. The case study from your first real engagement is worth more than any certification.

5
Month 8–18

Choose your route: independent or firm

Independent consulting is viable once you have 2–3 case studies and a referral network. Boutique AI consultancies are a good middle path — smaller than the Big 4, move faster, care less about credentials. Large consultancies (Accenture, Deloitte, KPMG all have active AI practices) are an option but often require degree screening at junior levels. Senior lateral hires with strong track records are evaluated differently.

FAQ

Common questions.

Start inside your existing network. Former colleagues, managers, clients from your previous role — these people already trust you. Offer a free AI opportunity assessment for their organisation. Run it properly. Document the output. That becomes your first case study. From there, referrals are the fastest growth channel in consulting.
If you've never worked in consulting, join a firm first — even a boutique one. Consulting has its own craft: scoping projects, managing client relationships, delivering under uncertainty, writing proposals. Learning that craft while being paid a salary, with senior support, is significantly less risky than learning it independently while burning savings. Go independent once you know the craft.
Rates vary enormously by specialisation and track record. Generalists with thin experience: $100–$200/hour. Domain specialists with clear positioning and 2–3 case studies: $200–$400/hour. Senior practitioners with a strong track record and referral network in a high-value industry like financial services or pharma: $400–$700/hour. The jump from generalist to specialist rates is the most important career move to make early.
Certifications help with credibility in early conversations — particularly with clients who are evaluating multiple consultants. Google Cloud's AI certification, AWS AI Practitioner, and the IAPP's AIGP are the most recognised. But they're a signal, not a substitute for a track record. Prioritise getting real engagements over accumulating certifications.
Follow the releases that matter to your domain specifically — not every AI development. Build something small every quarter, even if it's just a prototype using a new tool. The consultants who stay relevant are the ones who keep practicing, not just reading newsletters. Your clients will ask you about things you haven't heard of — knowing how to quickly evaluate unfamiliar tools is the core meta-skill.
