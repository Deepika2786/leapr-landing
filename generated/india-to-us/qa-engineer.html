Action Plan

Steps to land a US QA Engineer role from India.

1
Build Automation-Heavy Portfolio
Create 2-3 GitHub projects demonstrating Selenium, API testing with REST Assured or Postman, and CI/CD pipeline integration. US employers review code; Indian manual-testing experience alone is insufficient. Focus on real-world scenarios: e-commerce checkout, payment flows, user authentication.
2
Learn Programming for QA
Master Java or Python at a level where you can write reusable test frameworks, not just test scripts. Take a 4-8 week course on test automation frameworks. Practice coding interview problems on LeetCode (medium level). US interviews often include a 30-min coding challenge.
3
Get H-1B Visa Sponsorship Ready
Target companies known for sponsoring: Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Apple, Meta, Salesforce, Adobe, Atlassian. Check H-1B Tracker and LinkedIn for sponsorship patterns. Your salary expectation (₹80L+ equivalent ≈ $95K+) must meet Department of Labor's Prevailing Wage requirement. Smaller startups may sponsor; larger companies have clearer processes.
4
Optimize LinkedIn & Resume for US Market
Highlight automation frameworks, programming languages, and tools prominently. Replace 'QA Engineer' title history with specific tech stack (e.g., 'SDET - Selenium, Java, TestNG'). List testing tools: Jira, Jenkins, Git, AWS basics. Get recommendations from managers emphasizing technical depth and independent problem-solving.
5
Practice US-Style Technical Interviews
US QA interviews include: writing test automation code live, debugging failing tests, designing test strategies for new features, and behavioral questions (STAR method). Practice with mock interviews on Pramp or Interviewing.io. Prepare answers on how you've improved testing efficiency or prevented production bugs.
6
Target Companies & Apply Strategically
Apply to tech hubs: Silicon Valley, Seattle, Austin, NYC, Boston. Use LinkedIn, AngelList, Dice, and company career pages—not just recruiters. Mention visa sponsorship willingness in cover letter. Apply during peak hiring (Jan-March, Sept-Oct). Referrals increase callback rate by 3-5x.
FAQ

India to US QA Engineer — common questions.

Do US companies hire QA Engineers from India for H-1B sponsorship?
Yes. FAANG companies, fintech, and enterprise software actively sponsor H-1B for QA roles. However, you must meet the Prevailing Wage threshold ($95K+) and have skills matching US demand: strong automation, programming, and CI/CD experience. Mid-market startups may hesitate due to sponsorship cost ($3-5K) and processing time (6+ months).
What certifications do I need to move from India to US as QA Engineer?
ISTQB is helpful but not required. US employers prioritize demonstrated skills: a strong GitHub portfolio with 2+ automation projects, real-world test framework experience, and coding ability. SDET (Software Development Engineer in Test) certifications or bootcamp completion may strengthen your profile over pure ISTQB.
How much will I earn as a QA Engineer in the US vs India?
US QA Engineers earn $90K-$140K base salary; SDET roles reach $130K-$180K+. In India, experienced QA earns ₹10-20 LPA (≈$12-24K). The salary jump is 4-7x, but factor in higher US cost of living (rent, healthcare, taxes). After taxes and living expenses, actual discretionary income is 2-3x higher.
Is my manual testing experience from India valued in the US?
Partially. Manual testing experience shows domain knowledge and test thinking, but it's table stakes—not a differentiator. US employers assume you know how to test; they want to see automation coding skills. Reframe your India experience as 'test strategy & design' combined with 'automation implementation' to bridge the gap.
How long does the visa sponsorship process take?
H-1B sponsorship takes 6-12 months from job offer to visa stamp. Timeline: job offer → LMIA/labor condition approval (4-6 weeks) → H-1B petition filing (Sept-Oct) → lottery + approval (6-8 weeks) → visa interview at US embassy in India (2-4 weeks). Plan 8-12 months total. Some companies offer L-1 or other visas that move faster.
Which US cities hire the most QA Engineers?
San Francisco Bay Area (Google, Apple, Meta), Seattle (Amazon, Microsoft), Austin (Oracle, Apple, Tesla), NYC (fintech, media tech), and Boston (enterprise software). Remote roles are increasingly available. Check job density on LinkedIn by location to target high-demand areas.
What programming language should I learn for US QA roles?
Java and Python dominate. Java is preferred for enterprise/fintech; Python for startups and data-heavy roles. Learn one deeply (write 3-5 real frameworks), then pick up JavaScript for web automation. Avoid spreading too thin across 5+ languages—depth beats breadth in US interviews.
Can I transition from manual QA in India to SDET in the US?
Yes, and it's recommended. SDET roles (Software Development Engineer in Test) pay $130K-$200K and face less sponsorship friction than pure QA roles. Transition by: (1) learning programming deeply, (2) building a test automation framework portfolio, (3) applying to SDET roles, not QA roles. Takes 6-12 months of focused learning.
