Action Plan

Steps to land a US QA Engineer role from India.

1
Build a US-Aligned QA Skill Stack
Master Selenium WebDriver, Cypress, or Playwright. Learn REST API testing with Postman and backend automation. Get hands-on with CI/CD tools (Jenkins, GitHub Actions). US employers expect automation-first mindset and cross-functional testing knowledge. Dedicate 3–4 months to close skill gaps.
2
Target Sponsor-Willing Companies
Apply to tech companies known for H-1B sponsorship: Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, Cisco, Intel, Adobe, VMware, and startups in SF, Seattle, and Austin. Use LinkedIn filters for 'visa sponsorship' and check Glassdoor company reviews for sponsorship feedback. Avoid non-tech companies less likely to sponsor.
3
Optimize Your Resume for ATS & US Hiring
Format: single-page, no photo, no age/date of birth. Use metrics: 'Automated 2,000+ test cases, reducing manual testing by 60%' instead of 'worked on testing.' List tools explicitly (Selenium, Cypress, JIRA, Jenkins). Include GitHub link with test automation projects. Tailor for each JD.
4
Prepare for Technical & Behavioral Interviews
Expect 2–3 rounds: coding (write test scripts), automation framework design, and behavioral (tell me about a time you...). Practice on LeetCode (test automation scenarios), review STAR method for stories. US interviews favor problem-solving depth over rote knowledge. Practice answering in 2–3 minutes with clear examples.
5
Network & Land Referrals
Referrals bypass visa concerns for many companies. Join LinkedIn groups for Indian QA engineers in the US, attend virtual tech meetups, engage in GitHub/testing communities. Reach out to recruiters specializing in H-1B placement. A referral from an insider increases offer chances by 40%+.
6
Understand the H-1B Process & Timeline
H-1B lottery opens April; results in May; visa interview in summer; start date typically October. Plan 6–9 months from job offer to US arrival. Salary must meet USCIS prevailing wage ($90K+ for QA). Employer covers visa fees (~$2,000–$3,000). Be transparent about visa needs upfront.
FAQ

India to US QA Engineer — common questions.

Do I need to be in the US to apply for QA Engineer jobs?
No. Many US companies hire remote initially, especially for visa sponsorship cases. However, most expect relocation within 3–6 months post-offer. Start applying from India now; visa sponsorship is handled post-offer.
How long does H-1B sponsorship take?
H-1B filing opens April 1, lottery held in May, approval by July, visa interview in summer, and work start by October. Total timeline: 6–9 months from offer to US arrival. Plan your transition accordingly.
What's the realistic salary jump from India to the US?
If earning ₹12 LPA (~$144k USD equivalent at current rates), expect $95–$130K USD as a mid-level QA Engineer. Gross salary seems lower, but after-tax take-home is similar due to lower US tax rates, plus benefits (health, 401(k), stock options) add 20–30% value.
Will my 5 years of QA experience in India count as 5 years in the US?
Partially. US employers value automation-first experience. If you've done manual testing + minimal automation, expect to be leveled as 3–4 years in the US. Upskill in Selenium, API testing, and CI/CD to bridge the gap and justify your current level.
Which US cities are best for QA Engineer jobs with visa sponsorship?
San Francisco/Bay Area, Seattle, Austin, New York, Boston, and Sunnyvale. Tech hubs have the most sponsorship-friendly companies. However, remote work has expanded opportunities; many companies now hire QA Engineers anywhere in the US.
Do I need ISTQB or other certifications?
Not required. US companies prioritize hands-on automation skills over certifications. ISTQB can help with foundational knowledge but won't compensate for weak automation skills. Focus on building a strong GitHub portfolio of test automation projects instead.
What if my English accent is a concern?
Clear communication matters more than accent. Practice speaking in technical interviews, use structured answers (STAR method), and be confident. Many successful Indian QA Engineers work in the US; hiring managers expect diverse accents. Clarity and technical depth speak louder.
