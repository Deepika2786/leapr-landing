Common questions

Sales Manager → Data Scientist FAQ

Can I become a data scientist without a math degree?
Yes. You'll need statistics fundamentals (not advanced math), which you can learn in 2–3 months. Online courses cover what you need. Many working data scientists came from non-technical backgrounds. Your sales background is actually an asset—you ask better business questions.
Do I need to quit my job to make this transition?
Not necessarily. Most career changers study part-time (6–12 months) while employed. This keeps income stable and gives you real data to build portfolio projects with. You can interview while still managing your team, then give notice after landing a role.
Will I take a pay cut transitioning to junior data scientist?
Typically no—junior data scientists in tech hubs earn $85k–$120k. As a sales manager, you're likely in that range or higher. Entry-level roles in lower-cost regions pay $65k–$85k. Your domain expertise can command higher entry salaries at companies valuing sales-side analytics.
What's the fastest path: bootcamp or self-study?
Self-study is slower (8–14 months, part-time) but cheaper and lets you stay employed. Bootcamps are faster (3–4 months, full-time) but cost $10k–$20k and require you to leave your job. Hybrid: take online courses for 4–5 months, then a 2-month bootcamp to accelerate interviews.
Should I get a data science certification or just build projects?
Projects matter more. Employers want to see code on GitHub and results on real datasets. Certifications (Google, IBM) add credibility but aren't required. Focus 80% effort on 3–4 strong portfolio projects and 20% on a recognized certificate if you want one.
