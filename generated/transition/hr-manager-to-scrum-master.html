Month 3–5
Facilitate Real Sprints & Retrospectives
Move into an official Scrum Master role on a real team. Run daily standups, sprint planning, and retrospectives. Track velocity and burndown charts. Use your conflict resolution skills to unblock team tensions, but focus on process improvement over people management—this is the mental shift from HR to Scrum.

Common questions

HR Manager → Scrum Master FAQ

Do I need a CSM or PSM certification to become a Scrum Master?
Most tech teams expect it. CSM (2-day, ~$300–500) and PSM I (self-study, ~$150 exam) are industry-standard. Without one, you'll struggle to be taken seriously by engineering leads. Get certified in month 1–2 of your transition.
Will my HR salary transfer to a Scrum Master role?
Not directly. Scrum Master roles typically pay 5–15% less than mid-level HR Manager roles because they're usually entry to mid-level in tech. However, as you gain credibility and move to Senior Scrum Master or scale to multiple teams, you'll recover the gap within 18–24 months.
What's the biggest mindset shift from HR Manager to Scrum Master?
HR is about people first; Scrum is about process first. You're not solving interpersonal problems—you're removing blockers to team delivery. Your conflict resolution will still help, but only within the framework of sprint goals and retrospectives.
Can I transition without technical background?
Yes, but you need willingness to learn. You don't code, but you must understand SDLC basics, deployment pipelines, and why technical debt matters. Spend 2–3 months on fundamentals before joining a real team.
How long before I'm competitive for a Scrum Master job?
5–8 months with active learning and hands-on facilitation of at least 3–4 real sprints. Your HR skills get you the interview; your agile credentials and sprint experience get you hired.
