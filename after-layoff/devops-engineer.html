First 7 days

What to do
this week.

DevOps engineers are recruited heavily through LinkedIn and GitHub. Your visibility on both platforms matters more than your resume in the first week.

Day 1–2

File unemployment and calculate your runway

Do this first. Knowing exactly how long you can search without financial pressure lets you be selective rather than desperate. Most bad hire decisions happen when candidates run out of runway and take the first offer available.

Day 2–3

Update LinkedIn with your specific stack

Recruiters search for exact tool names — "Kubernetes," "Terraform," "GitHub Actions," "ArgoCD." Your headline should list your actual stack. Being specific means being findable by the right people.

Day 3–5

Make your GitHub active and public

Review your GitHub profile — is there recent activity? Is your infrastructure code public? Add a README to your most significant project explaining what it does and why you made the architecture decisions you did. DevOps hiring managers look at GitHub before they read your resume.

Day 5–7

Message 15 former colleagues directly

Personalised messages to specific people in your network — not a broadcast. DevOps and platform engineering is a relationship-driven community. A referral from someone who's worked with you is worth more than 20 cold applications.

Your assets

What you have that
the market wants.

Highest demand

Kubernetes and container orchestration

CKA-certified engineers or those with significant production Kubernetes experience are consistently among the most sought-after DevOps candidates. If you have it, surface it in your headline and quantify the scale — number of clusters, node count, workloads managed.

High demand

Infrastructure as Code — Terraform

Terraform is the standard IaC tool in most modern environments. Experience managing Terraform state at scale, writing reusable modules, and handling drift detection is specifically valued. Mention the cloud provider and the scope of infrastructure you managed.

Strong signal

CI/CD pipeline design and maintenance

GitHub Actions, GitLab CI, and Jenkins experience are baseline expectations. What differentiates candidates is pipeline complexity — multi-stage builds, environment promotion strategies, test parallelisation, security scanning integration. Describe yours specifically.

Growing fast

Platform engineering and developer experience

Companies are increasingly hiring Platform Engineers who build internal developer platforms. If you've worked on developer tooling, self-service infrastructure, or internal platforms — this is a growing and well-compensated specialisation worth targeting.

90-day plan

Your roadmap to
next offer.

1
Week 1–2

Activate your network and get visible on GitHub and LinkedIn

LinkedIn updated with specific tools. GitHub profile cleaned up with active public projects. 15 personalised network messages sent. 5 targeted applications submitted. Leapr analysis complete.

2
Week 2–5

Apply to companies with modern DevOps practices

Target companies whose job descriptions mention Kubernetes, ArgoCD, Terraform, and GitHub Actions — not JIRA and Jenkins. The tools signal the maturity of the environment. Apply 15–20 times per week with tailored resumes that mirror each job description's specific tool names.

3
Week 3–6

Prepare for technical interviews with specific stories

DevOps interviews ask about real incidents — "Walk me through the worst outage you've dealt with." Prepare 3 specific incident stories: what happened, how you diagnosed it, how you contained it, what you changed to prevent recurrence. Specificity — tools used, timeline, user impact — is what moves you from "candidate" to "hire."

4
Week 4–8

Build parallel processes and create leverage

5–8 active processes simultaneously. When one reaches offer stage, use it to accelerate others. Never accept or decline an offer on the same day you receive it — always ask for time to consider.

5
Week 6–12

Negotiate your full package

Remote flexibility, on-call compensation, equity, and professional development budget are all negotiable. DevOps engineers who are strong on-call are genuinely valuable — that value should be reflected in your compensation. Counter with specifics, not ranges.

What not to do

Mistakes that
cost you weeks.

Applying to roles that primarily use legacy tooling

Jenkins-only shops, on-premise infrastructure roles, and environments that haven't adopted containerisation will slow your career growth. Use your search to upgrade your environment, not just replace your salary.

Leaving your GitHub empty or private

A DevOps engineer with no public GitHub presence is harder to evaluate than one with even a small project demonstrating their approach to infrastructure. Add at least one public Terraform project or CI/CD workflow during your first week.

Not preparing for the on-call and incident response questions

Every DevOps interview asks about incidents. Candidates who say "I've handled lots of incidents" without a specific story lose to candidates who can walk through one precise incident in detail.

Evaluating offers only on base salary

On-call rotation expectations, incident frequency, team size, and remote flexibility all affect quality of life significantly in DevOps roles. A role paying 10% more with a brutal on-call rotation is often worse than a slightly lower-paying role with a sustainable one.
