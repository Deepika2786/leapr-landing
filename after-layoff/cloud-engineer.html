First 7 days

What to do
this week.

The first week matters more than people think — not because of urgency, but because the actions you take now determine whether you spend 6 weeks or 6 months in search. Here's what actually moves the needle.

Day 1–2

Get your financial runway clear

File for unemployment immediately — most states have a waiting period, so every day you delay costs you. Calculate your exact monthly burn. Know your number. This removes the panic from your decision-making and lets you be selective rather than desperate.

Day 2–3

Update your LinkedIn before anything else

Add "Open to Work" (visible to recruiters only, not your network if you prefer). Update your headline to your strongest credential — AWS Solutions Architect | Cloud Infrastructure | Terraform. Recruiters search LinkedIn daily. Being findable is step one.

Day 3–5

Audit your certifications and refresh what's lapsing

AWS, Azure, and GCP certifications expire. Check your expiry dates now. If any are within 6 months, renew them before starting your search — a lapsed certification on your resume creates doubt. A current certification stack is your most immediate credential upgrade.

Day 5–7

Reach out to your network — specifically, not broadly

Message 10–15 former colleagues, managers, and professional contacts directly. Not "I'm looking for opportunities" — specific: "I'm exploring cloud engineering roles with a focus on AWS/Terraform. If you know anyone at [company], I'd love a warm intro." Referrals close 4x faster than cold applications.

90-day plan

Your roadmap to
next offer.

1
Week 1–2

Optimise your presence and activate your network

LinkedIn headline updated. Resume tailored to target role level. 15 personalised network messages sent. 3 cloud consultancies applied to directly. Leapr analysis done so you know exactly where your gaps are.

2
Week 2–4

Apply strategically, not broadly

15–20 targeted applications per week beats 50 spray-and-pray applications. For each application, customise your resume to mirror the job description's language — especially specific services (EKS vs GKE, Terraform vs Pulumi). ATS systems score keyword match before a human sees your resume.

3
Week 3–6

Nail the technical interview format

Cloud engineering interviews typically include: system design (design a multi-region highly available architecture), troubleshooting scenarios (a Lambda is timing out — walk me through your debugging approach), and IaC questions (how do you manage Terraform state across teams). Practice these specifically — they're predictable and preparable.

4
Week 4–8

Build leverage with parallel tracks

Don't wait for offers sequentially. Keep 5–8 active processes running simultaneously. When one company moves to offer stage, use it to accelerate others. "I have another offer I need to respond to by Friday" is legitimate leverage that consistently works.

5
Week 6–12

Negotiate — cloud engineers consistently leave money on the table

The first offer is rarely the best offer. Counter with a specific number — not a range. Know your BATNA (best alternative). Cloud engineers with strong certification stacks and production experience have more leverage than most candidates in the market right now.

What not to do

Mistakes that
cost you weeks.

Spending week 1 updating your resume instead of talking to people

Your resume matters but referrals close faster than any application. The first week should be 80% network activation, 20% resume polish. Most people do the opposite.

Applying to roles that are a level too senior or too junior

Job titles vary wildly by company. "Senior Cloud Engineer" at a startup might mean 3 years experience. At AWS it means 8+. Read the requirements carefully and apply at your actual level — not your aspirational one.

Letting certifications lapse during your search

An expired AWS certification in your search period signals carelessness. Check expiry dates on day one and renew anything within 6 months before your resume goes out.

Accepting the first offer out of anxiety

The layoff anxiety pushes people to take the first offer they get. If it comes in week 3, you almost certainly have better options that just haven't materialised yet. Counter, buy time if you can, and keep your pipeline moving.
