First 7 days

What to do
this week.

Full stack developer roles fill through GitHub visibility, recruiter outreach, and referrals — in roughly that order. The first week is about getting your technical presence visible and your network activated, not polishing your resume.

Day 1–2

File unemployment and know your runway

Do this immediately. Full stack searches typically take 5–10 weeks — knowing your financial runway removes desperation from your decision-making and lets you hold out for the right role rather than the first one.

Day 2–3

Audit your GitHub — this is your resume

Is your best project public with a clear README? Is there recent commit activity? Hiring managers look at your GitHub before they read your CV. Add a professional README to your top 2 repositories explaining what the project does, the tech stack, and key decisions you made.

Day 3–5

Update LinkedIn with your specific stack

Generic "React, Node.js, PostgreSQL" is better than nothing but still generic. "Built multi-tenant SaaS platform serving 50k users — React, TypeScript, Node.js, PostgreSQL, deployed on AWS" is specific and memorable. Be exact about scale and impact.

Day 5–7

Message 15 former colleagues and technical contacts

Personalised messages about specific companies or roles — not broadcast "I'm looking" posts. Former managers and tech leads who've seen your work are your most powerful advocates. A warm referral at a startup often skips 2 interview rounds.

Your assets

What you have that
the market wants.

Highest demand

TypeScript experience

TypeScript adoption has accelerated massively. Engineers who can work in TypeScript on both frontend (React/Next.js) and backend (Node.js) are specifically sought. If you have it, make it the first thing on your resume — not buried in a skills list.

High demand

Production experience at scale

Having shipped features used by real users at meaningful scale — even 10k MAU — is a significant differentiator from developers whose experience is primarily tutorial projects. Quantify your production exposure wherever possible.

Strong signal

AI feature integration experience

Full stack developers who've integrated LLM APIs, built AI-powered features, or worked with vector databases are in strong demand from AI-first companies. Even personal project experience with OpenAI or Anthropic APIs is worth surfacing.

Differentiator

Ownership mentality — shipping end to end

Startups want developers who can own a feature from design to deployment — not developers who need to hand off to separate teams for every layer. Evidence of full ownership (designed, built, deployed, monitored) is what startup hiring managers are explicitly looking for.

Most active

Series A–C startups

Growth-stage startups (10–150 engineers) are the most active full stack hirers. They move fast, give real ownership, and their equity can be meaningful. Target companies that have raised in the last 18 months — they have runway and hiring mandates.

Active hiring

AI-first product companies

Companies building AI-powered products need full stack developers who can integrate LLM APIs and build AI UX. Even basic LLM integration experience gives you access to this fast-growing hiring pool.

Reliable option

Agencies and development studios

Digital agencies and product studios hire full stack developers continuously. The work is varied, the hiring bar is lower than product companies, and you accumulate diverse project experience quickly. A good bridge while waiting for the right permanent role.

Remote availability

Strong remote market

74% of full stack roles are remote or hybrid. Full stack is one of the most remote-friendly engineering disciplines. Your geographic constraints are minimal — cast a wide net.

90-day plan

Your roadmap to
next offer.

1
Week 1–2

GitHub and LinkedIn visible, network activated

GitHub READMEs updated. LinkedIn updated with specific stack and scale. 15 personalised network messages sent. 5 targeted applications to Series A–C companies. Leapr analysis done so you know exactly where to focus.

2
Week 2–5

Apply strategically to companies using your stack

Target companies whose job descriptions list your exact stack — React + TypeScript + Node.js + PostgreSQL. Customise every resume to mirror the exact framework names listed. 15–20 applications per week. Quality beats volume — a tailored application to a relevant company beats 5 generic ones.

3
Week 3–6

Prepare for take-home projects and system design

Most startup full stack interviews include a take-home project. Treat it seriously — clear code, proper error handling, a README, deployed to a URL. The candidate who deploys a working version wins over the candidate with cleaner code that requires running locally. Prepare to walk through your architecture decisions.

4
Week 4–8

Build parallel processes — 5 active at once

Never have fewer than 5 active processes. When one reaches offer stage, immediately contact all others to accelerate. "I'm evaluating another offer" is honest leverage if your pipeline is real.

5
Week 6–10

Negotiate on base, equity, and remote flexibility

At startups, equity negotiation is often more valuable than base salary negotiation. Understand the company's last valuation, the option pool size, and your percentage before evaluating the package. A 0.1% stake at a well-funded Series B is meaningful. Remote flexibility and async culture are also negotiable — ask explicitly.

What not to do

Mistakes that
cost you weeks.

Treating LeetCode preparation as your primary activity

Startups — your best target market — rarely run LeetCode-style interviews. They want to see a take-home project or a practical coding exercise. Spending 4 hours a day on LeetCode when your target market doesn't test it is a significant misallocation of your search time.

Only applying to large tech companies

FAANG and large tech companies move slowly, have high bars, and are cutting headcount — not the best target for a fast search. Startups and scaleups move in days, not weeks, and value full stack ownership more than algorithmic purity.

Submitting take-home projects without deploying them

A project that requires "npm install && npm start" to evaluate is at a significant disadvantage against a project with a live URL. Deploy every take-home to Vercel or Railway before submitting. It takes 10 minutes and separates you from 60% of other candidates.

Undervaluing your production experience

If you've shipped features to real users, describe the scale specifically. "Built user authentication system" is forgettable. "Built authentication system for 80k users with OAuth, MFA, and session management" is memorable and gives interviewers something to ask about.
