First 7 days

What to do
this week.

Data engineering roles are filled primarily through two channels — recruiter outreach to visible LinkedIn profiles, and referrals from former colleagues. Both require you to be active immediately, not after you've finished updating your resume.

Day 1–2

File unemployment and calculate your runway

Do this before anything else. Knowing your exact financial runway removes the desperation that leads to accepting the wrong offer. Most candidates who take poor offers do so in the first 3 weeks before their pipeline is properly built.

Day 2–3

Update your LinkedIn with specific stack details

Recruiters search for specific tools — "dbt," "Snowflake," "Airflow," "Spark." Your headline should list your actual stack: "Data Engineer | dbt | Snowflake | Airflow | Python." Being specific is being findable.

Day 3–5

Message 15 former colleagues directly

Not a broadcast message — personalised notes to specific people. "I'm looking for data engineering roles with a focus on the modern data stack (dbt, Snowflake). If you hear of anything at your company or network, I'd appreciate the heads up." Referrals close in half the time of cold applications.

Day 5–7

Check your dbt and Snowflake profile visibility

dbt has a community Slack with 50,000+ data professionals. Snowflake has a community site. Being visible and active in these communities puts you in front of hiring managers who monitor them. A useful comment in #jobs-and-hiring is worth 10 cold applications.

Your assets

What you have that
the market wants.

The modern data stack transition is still in progress at most companies. Engineers who've worked with dbt, cloud warehouses, and orchestration tools in production are genuinely scarce relative to demand.

Highest demand

dbt experience

dbt appears in a majority of modern data engineering job descriptions. If you have production dbt experience — building models, writing tests, managing documentation — this is your strongest differentiator. Lead with it.

High demand

Cloud data warehouse expertise

Snowflake, BigQuery, and Redshift experience is expected at most data companies. If you have performance tuning, cost optimisation, or data modelling depth in any of these, quantify the impact — "reduced query costs by 40%" is a specific, memorable claim.

Strong signal

Pipeline orchestration — Airflow or Dagster

Airflow experience is nearly universal in data engineering roles. If you've built and maintained production DAGs, mentioning specific complexity — number of DAGs, SLA requirements, failure handling — makes your experience concrete.

Growing fast

Real-time and streaming experience

Kafka, Flink, and Spark Streaming experience commands a premium and significantly expands your role options. If you have streaming experience, it's worth a dedicated section on your resume.

90-day plan

Your roadmap to
next offer.

1
Week 1–2

Activate your network and get your profile visible

LinkedIn updated with specific tools. dbt Slack community joined and active. 15 personalised network messages sent. 5 targeted applications submitted to data-forward companies. Leapr analysis done so you know exactly where your gaps are versus the market.

2
Week 2–5

Apply strategically — modern stack companies first

Target companies that list dbt, Snowflake, or Airflow specifically in job descriptions — they're most likely to value your existing skills without requiring you to learn their proprietary stack from scratch. Customise each resume to mirror the exact tool names in the job description.

3
Week 3–6

Prepare your technical interview stories

Data engineering interviews typically include: a SQL problem (window functions, CTEs), a system design question (design a data pipeline for X use case), and a portfolio walkthrough. For the portfolio, prepare specific stories about pipelines you've built — the business problem, your design decisions, the scale, and what you'd do differently.

4
Week 4–8

Run parallel processes and build leverage

5–8 active interview processes simultaneously. When one reaches offer stage, use it to accelerate others. "I have an offer with a response deadline" is legitimate leverage that consistently works — even at companies that claim their process "doesn't move faster."

5
Week 6–12

Negotiate your total package — not just base salary

Remote flexibility, equity, signing bonus, and professional development budget are all negotiable. Data engineers in the modern stack have leverage — use it. Counter with a specific number, not a range. A range tells the employer to pay you the bottom of it.

What not to do

Mistakes that
cost you weeks.

Applying to roles that use legacy tooling when you know the modern stack

SSIS, Informatica, and on-premise Hadoop roles pay less, grow slower, and waste your modern stack skills. Target companies where your dbt and Snowflake experience is valued, not companies where you'd be learning outdated patterns.

Generic SQL on your resume when you have advanced experience

"Proficient in SQL" is invisible. "Built 200+ dbt models serving 15 analysts, reduced average query time by 60% through materialisation strategy" is specific and memorable. Quantify everything.

Skipping the SQL interview prep because you do it every day

Interview SQL problems are different from production SQL — they're time-pressured, whiteboard-style, and often test specific patterns. Practice on StrataScratch and DataLemur for 30 minutes daily regardless of your actual SQL fluency.

Ignoring Analytics Engineer titles as "below" Data Engineer

Analytics Engineer roles frequently pay comparably to Data Engineer roles and are easier to land if your experience skews toward transformation over pipeline infrastructure. Don't let title pride narrow your options unnecessarily.

FAQ

Questions you're
probably asking.

It depends on how you present it. Legacy experience doesn't disqualify you — but you need to demonstrate modern stack awareness. During your search, spin up a free Snowflake trial and build a small dbt project. Put it on GitHub. Being able to say "my production experience is with X, but I've been building with dbt and Snowflake in my own time" combined with a GitHub link removes most objections.
Snowflake's SnowPro Core certification is respected and takes 4–6 weeks to prepare for. If you're not already Snowflake-certified and Snowflake appears prominently in your target job descriptions, it's worth pursuing during your search. Databricks certifications are worth it if you're targeting Spark/ML pipeline roles specifically. Don't let certification pursuit delay active applications — do both in parallel.
Fill the gap with something visible — a personal data project on GitHub, a freelance analytics engagement, a contribution to an open source data tool, or a certification course. Data hiring managers understand layoffs. What they're less comfortable with is a gap with nothing to show for it. Even a well-documented side project signals that you stayed sharp.
Data engineering contract rates are strong — typically $80–$130/hour depending on specialisation and location. A 3–6 month contract fills your resume gap, keeps your skills current, generates income, and occasionally converts to permanent. If you're approaching month 2 without strong leads, seriously consider contract roles as a parallel track rather than a fallback.
