The role

What does a AI Engineer
actually do?

An AI Engineer builds products and systems powered by artificial intelligence. Unlike ML researchers who create new models, AI Engineers take existing models — especially large language models — and build real applications on top of them. You're the bridge between cutting-edge AI research and working software.

This is the fastest-growing engineering role of the 2020s. Companies are desperate for engineers who can take LLMs like GPT-4 or Claude and build reliable, production-ready systems. The role combines software engineering, prompt engineering, and enough ML knowledge to make good architectural decisions.

You will do a lot of

Building LLM-powered applications

RAG systems, AI agents, chatbots, document processing — taking foundation models and making them useful for specific use cases.

You will do a lot of

Evaluation and reliability

Making AI systems that actually work reliably in production — not just impressive demos.

You will do less of

Training models from scratch

That's ML research. AI Engineers use existing foundation models and fine-tune them when needed.

You will do less of

Pure frontend work

AI Engineering is primarily backend and systems work, though full-stack AI engineers are increasingly valuable.

The path

Your step-by-step
roadmap.

This is the typical path for someone transitioning into ai engineer. Your actual timeline depends on your starting point and how much time you invest.

1
Month 1–2

Master Python and LLM APIs

Python is essential. Then get hands-on with the OpenAI and Anthropic APIs immediately — build something that calls an LLM within your first week. Read the documentation thoroughly. Understand tokens, context windows, and cost management.

2
Month 2–4

Build a RAG system

Retrieval Augmented Generation is the most in-demand AI engineering skill right now. Build a system that can answer questions about a document corpus using embeddings and vector search. This one project covers 80% of what most companies need.

3
Month 3–6

Learn evaluation and production patterns

AI systems fail in subtle ways. Learn how to evaluate LLM outputs systematically. Study patterns like chains, agents, and tool use. Deploy something to production — even a simple side project. Production experience is rare and highly valued.

4
Month 5–9

Build an AI agent

Agents that can use tools, browse the web, write and execute code are the frontier of AI engineering. Build one. Document what broke, how you fixed it, and what you learned. This is the portfolio piece that will get you interviews.

5
Month 8–15

Target AI Engineer roles

The market is hot. Companies are hiring AI Engineers from software engineering, data science, and even non-technical backgrounds. Your domain knowledge from a previous career is a massive advantage — healthcare AI, legal AI, and fintech AI all need people who understand the domain.
