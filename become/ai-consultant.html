The role

What does a AI Consultant
actually do?

An AI Consultant advises organisations on how to adopt, implement, and benefit from artificial intelligence. You assess where AI creates value (and where it doesn't), design AI strategies, evaluate vendor solutions, and guide implementation — without necessarily building the AI systems yourself.

This is fundamentally a business role with a technical edge. The best AI consultants are expert at separating genuine AI value from hype, at understanding the organisational change required to make AI work, and at translating complex AI concepts into business language that executives can act on.

You will do a lot of

AI strategy and opportunity assessment

Identifying where AI genuinely creates business value for a specific organisation — and where it doesn't.

You will do a lot of

Vendor evaluation and selection

Assessing AI tools, platforms, and models objectively — helping clients make informed build-vs-buy decisions.

You will do less of

Building AI systems

That's AI Engineering. Consultants advise; engineers build.

You will do less of

Ongoing operations

Consultants typically engage for projects, not long-term operations. Managed services is a different model.

The path

Your step-by-step
roadmap.

This is the typical path for someone transitioning into ai consultant. Your actual timeline depends on your starting point and how much time you invest.

1
Month 1–3

Build deep AI literacy

You need to genuinely understand AI — not just the buzzwords. Build with LLM APIs. Understand how ML models work. Read widely on AI applications in your domain. Take a technical AI course even if you're not going to become an engineer. Consultants who've built something command more respect.

2
Month 2–5

Develop your domain specialisation

The most valuable AI consultants combine AI knowledge with deep domain expertise. Healthcare AI, legal AI, financial services AI, manufacturing AI — these require someone who understands both the technology and the industry. Your previous career is your advantage.

3
Month 3–7

Build advisory frameworks

Develop your approach to AI opportunity assessment, ROI calculation, vendor evaluation, and implementation planning. Clients pay for structured thinking, not just information. Your frameworks are your intellectual property.

4
Month 5–10

Get your first client

Offer pro bono advisory to a non-profit or small business. Write detailed AI strategy pieces publicly. Speak at industry events. Consulting requires a track record — get experience however you can, even if unpaid initially.

5
Month 8–18

Target consulting roles or build a practice

Large consultancies (Deloitte, McKinsey, Accenture, KPMG) all have AI practices and hire domain experts with AI knowledge. Boutique AI consultancies are another option. Independent consulting is viable once you have a track record and network. Rates range from $200 to $500+/hour for experienced AI consultants.

FAQ

Common questions.

You need AI literacy — enough to evaluate solutions, spot bad advice, and have credible conversations with technical teams. You don't need to build AI systems. The business and domain expertise is often more valuable than deep technical skill for consulting. But consultants who've built something have significantly more credibility.
The supply of people claiming AI expertise far exceeds those with genuine depth. The market is crowded with generalists and thin on specialists. Deep domain expertise combined with genuine AI knowledge is genuinely rare — that's the gap to target.
Start at a consultancy if you're new to consulting — you'll learn the craft, build a network, and have a brand behind you. Go independent once you have a track record, a network, and a clear specialisation. Independent AI consultants with genuine expertise can command very high rates.
Build things regularly — even small experiments. Follow AI research (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind all publish extensively). Attend AI conferences. The consultants who stay relevant are the ones who keep practicing, not just reading.
Healthcare, legal, financial services, manufacturing, and retail are all undergoing significant AI transformation and need advisors who understand both the technology and the industry. Government and public sector are also significant buyers of AI advisory services.
