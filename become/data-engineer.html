The role

What does a Data Engineer
actually do?

A Data Engineer builds and maintains the infrastructure that makes data usable. You design pipelines that move data from source systems into warehouses, ensure data quality, and make sure analysts and data scientists have clean, reliable data to work with.

The job is fundamentally about plumbing — but intelligent, scalable plumbing. A broken pipeline can bring a business's decision-making to a halt. The best data engineers are obsessive about reliability, documentation, and making their systems invisible because everything just works.

You will do a lot of

Building data pipelines

Moving data from source systems (APIs, databases, event streams) to analytical destinations reliably and at scale.

You will do a lot of

Data modelling

Designing how data is structured, stored, and queried — decisions that affect every downstream user.

You will do less of

Ad-hoc analysis

That's more data analyst territory. Your job is to make analysis possible, not to do it yourself.

You will do less of

Product decisions

You build the infrastructure layer. Business and product questions are handled upstream.

The path

Your step-by-step
roadmap.

This is the typical path for someone transitioning into data engineer. Your actual timeline depends on your starting point and how much time you invest.

1
Month 1–2

Master advanced SQL and Python

SQL is the foundation. Go beyond SELECT — learn window functions, CTEs, and query optimisation. Python for data manipulation with pandas. These two skills alone can get you an entry-level role.

2
Month 2–4

Learn a data warehouse and dbt

Pick one: BigQuery, Snowflake, or Redshift. Learn dbt to transform data within the warehouse. This combination is the most in-demand skill set in data engineering right now.

3
Month 3–6

Build a pipeline project

Create an end-to-end pipeline: ingest data from a public API, clean it, load it into a warehouse, model it with dbt, and visualise it. Document everything. This becomes the centrepiece of your portfolio.

4
Month 5–9

Learn orchestration and streaming

Airflow for batch orchestration. Kafka or Pub/Sub for streaming. These are the tools that separate junior from mid-level data engineers.

5
Month 8–18

Target your first role

Start-ups are the best entry point — you'll touch every part of the stack. Mid-size companies with growing data teams are also good. Avoid companies where 'data engineer' means 'SQL report writer'.
