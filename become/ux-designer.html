The role

What does a UX Designer
actually do?

A UX Designer makes digital products easy and enjoyable to use. You research user needs, map out user journeys, design wireframes and prototypes, and work with product managers and engineers to ship experiences that solve real problems.

The best UX designers are fundamentally researchers who can also make things. They obsess about why users struggle, not just how the interface looks. The visual side matters — but understanding human behaviour is the real foundation.

You will do a lot of

User research

Interviews, usability tests, surveys — understanding what users actually need vs what they say they need.

You will do a lot of

Wireframing and prototyping

Translating insights into flows, wireframes, and clickable prototypes for testing and handoff.

You will do less of

Writing code

You'll work closely with engineers but UX designers don't typically write production code. Knowing HTML/CSS basics helps.

You will do less of

Visual branding

That's more graphic design or brand design. UX focuses on usability and flow, not brand aesthetics.

The path

Your step-by-step
roadmap.

This is the typical path for someone transitioning into ux designer. Your actual timeline depends on your starting point and how much time you invest.

1
Month 1–2

Learn Figma and UX fundamentals

Figma is the industry standard — learn it properly. Read 'The Design of Everyday Things' by Don Norman. Take Google's UX Design Certificate on Coursera for the fundamentals. Understand the double diamond design process.

2
Month 2–4

Conduct real user research

Run at least 10 user interviews for any product you care about. Document your findings. Identify patterns. This is the skill most career changers underestimate — and the one that makes you genuinely useful.

3
Month 3–5

Redesign something real

Take an existing app that frustrates you. Research why users struggle with it. Redesign a specific flow — not the whole app. Document your process: research findings, design decisions, trade-offs. This is your first case study.

4
Month 4–7

Build a 3-case-study portfolio

Each case study should follow the same structure: the problem, your research, your design decisions, the outcome or what you'd measure. Quality over quantity — three strong case studies beat ten weak ones.

5
Month 6–12

Target your first role

Junior UX Designer or UX Researcher roles are the right entry point. Agency roles offer variety; in-house roles offer depth. Your domain experience from your previous career is a genuine advantage — healthcare, finance, education all need designers who understand the domain.
