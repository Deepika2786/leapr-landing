The role

What does a Solutions Architect
actually do?

A Solutions Architect designs the technical architecture of systems — making high-level decisions about how components fit together, which technologies to use, and how to balance trade-offs between cost, performance, scalability, and maintainability.

The role exists in two main forms: internal (designing systems for your own company's products) and external (at cloud vendors or consultancies, designing solutions for client companies). Both require deep technical knowledge plus the ability to communicate complex decisions to non-technical stakeholders.

You will do a lot of

Architecture design

Creating technical blueprints — system diagrams, data flows, API contracts, and technology decisions that balance business requirements with technical constraints.

You will do a lot of

Stakeholder communication

Explaining complex technical decisions to non-technical audiences and translating business requirements into technical specifications.

You will do less of

Writing production code

Architects design systems; engineers build them. You'll write proof-of-concept code but not production implementation.

You will do less of

Day-to-day operations

That's for engineering and DevOps teams. Architects focus on design decisions, not ongoing maintenance.

The path

Your step-by-step
roadmap.

This is the typical path for someone transitioning into solutions architect. Your actual timeline depends on your starting point and how much time you invest.

1
Month 1–3

Deepen system design knowledge

Read 'Designing Data-Intensive Applications' by Martin Kleppmann — it's the definitive book. Study common patterns: caching, message queues, event sourcing, CQRS, API gateway patterns. Practice system design interviews even if you're not interviewing — it builds the right mental models.

2
Month 3–6

Get cloud certified

AWS Solutions Architect Professional or GCP Professional Cloud Architect are the most recognised credentials for this role. They validate the breadth of knowledge the role requires. The study process also fills in knowledge gaps.

3
Month 5–9

Build architecture documentation skills

Learn to create clear architecture diagrams (C4 model is excellent). Practice writing Architecture Decision Records (ADRs). Document systems you've built or worked on — the process of explaining them clearly is itself a skill.

4
Month 7–12

Seek architecture-adjacent opportunities

Technical lead roles, senior engineering positions with architecture ownership, or consulting projects are the fastest path. Solutions Architect is rarely an entry-level title — it requires demonstrated depth.

5
Month 12–20

Target Solutions Architect roles

Cloud vendor SA roles (AWS, GCP, Azure) are excellent — high comp, huge learning, and exposure to hundreds of different architecture challenges. Internal SA roles at large enterprises are also strong. Consulting firms hire experienced engineers into architecture roles.
