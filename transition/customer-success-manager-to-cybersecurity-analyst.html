Common questions

Customer Success Manager → Cybersecurity Analyst FAQ

Can I move from Customer Success to Cybersecurity without a CS degree?
Yes. CompTIA Security+, CEH, or equivalent certifications are more valuable than a degree. Most entry-level SOC roles require 1–2 years of IT/security experience plus a cert, which you can build through labs, internships, and hands-on projects in 12–14 months.
What's the biggest skill gap I'll face?
Network architecture and system administration. You won't need deep coding, but understanding TCP/IP, DNS, firewalls, and operating systems is non-negotiable. Expect 4–6 months of focused study here; labs are more valuable than courses.
Will I take a pay cut moving from CSM to junior analyst?
Not typically. Entry-level security analysts earn $55–75k; experienced CSMs earn $70–95k. You may stay flat for 6 months, then jump +$15–25k within 18 months as you specialize. The trajectory favors security long-term.
Should I get Security+ or CEH first?
Security+ is easier, faster (6–8 weeks), and accepted everywhere. CEH is harder but more prestigious for penetration testing roles. Start with Security+; add CEH or OSCP after 12 months in a role if you want to specialize in offensive security.
How do I explain this pivot to a security hiring manager?
Frame it as: 'I've spent 5 years managing customer risk and security concerns. I realized I want to solve those problems on the technical side.' Point to specific incidents where you identified security gaps. Your CSM experience is an asset, not a liability.
