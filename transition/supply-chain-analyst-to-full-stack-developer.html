Common questions

Supply Chain Analyst → Full Stack Developer FAQ

Do I need a computer science degree to transition?
No. Bootcamp graduates and self-taught developers fill most junior roles. Your 2–4 years in supply chain actually help: you understand business logic and can talk to stakeholders. Focus on projects, not credentials.
How much of my supply chain knowledge is actually useful?
Very. Domain expertise is rare among junior devs. If you build an app solving a real supply chain problem (inventory optimization, demand forecasting), you'll stand out to employers in that vertical and command higher pay.
Should I focus on frontend or backend first?
Learn both as a full-stack developer, but start with frontend (React). It's more visual, gives faster feedback, and feels less abstract than backend architecture. Switch to backend around month 3.
What's the biggest risk in this transition?
Burnout from job-hunting rejection. Plan for 3–6 months of applications before your first offer. Build in public (Twitter, GitHub), contribute to open-source, and network with other junior devs. Landing your first role is the hard part; the second is easier.
Can I do this part-time while keeping my job?
Yes, but it takes 12–18 months instead of 6–9. You'll need 20+ hours weekly. Many people work nights and weekends for 6 months, save aggressively, then go full-time for the final sprint. Both paths work.
