Common questions

Nurse → Technical Program Manager FAQ

Do I need to learn to code to become a Technical Program Manager?
No. You need to understand software development cycles, architecture concepts, and how teams work—not write code yourself. Most TPMs are non-technical founders or former PMs who learned on the job. Your healthcare systems knowledge is more valuable.
Will my nursing background hurt my chances in tech?
Not if you reframe it. Nurses are rare in tech, and your crisis management, stakeholder coordination, and attention to detail are competitive advantages. Position yourself as someone who brings operational rigor and customer empathy to technical teams.
What's the salary difference between nurse and TPM?
Entry-level TPM roles typically pay $110k–$140k; experienced TPMs at larger companies earn $180k–$250k+. Nurses average $65k–$85k depending on specialty and location. The jump is real, especially after your first 2–3 years in tech.
Should I aim for healthcare-tech companies or general tech?
Both are viable. Healthcare tech companies value your domain expertise and may hire you faster. General tech companies offer faster growth and higher salaries. Start where your network is strongest; you can always move after 18 months.
How do I explain the career pivot in interviews?
Lead with curiosity and systems thinking. Say something like: 'I've spent X years optimizing patient care delivery. I realized the same principles—coordination, risk management, stakeholder alignment—drive software delivery. I'm excited to apply that at scale in tech.' Always connect it to impact, not escape.
