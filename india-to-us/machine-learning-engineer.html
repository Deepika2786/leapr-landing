Action Plan

Steps to land a US Machine Learning Engineer role from India.

1
Audit Your Resume for US Standards
Remove academic details, certifications, and long descriptions. Rewrite each role around quantified impact: 'Improved model latency by 40%' instead of 'Worked on ML pipeline optimization.' Include GitHub links and deployed projects. US recruiters spend 6 seconds scanning—make every line count.
2
Build a Sponsorship-Ready Portfolio
Create 2-3 end-to-end ML projects on GitHub: data collection → model training → deployment. Use AWS, GCP, or Azure. Companies want to see you can ship, not just train models. Include a deployed API or web app. This directly addresses visa sponsorship concerns.
3
Master US-Style System Design Interviews
US interviews emphasize designing ML systems at scale: 'Design a recommendation system for 1M users.' Indian interviews often skip this. Practice on LeetCode's ML interview section and Alex Xu's 'Machine Learning System Design' book. Study real systems (Netflix, Spotify, LinkedIn).
4
Target Visa-Sponsoring Companies Explicitly
Apply directly to FAANG, Scale AI, Anthropic, OpenAI, Databricks, and similar companies known for H-1B sponsorship. Avoid small startups unless they explicitly confirm sponsorship. Use LinkedIn filters: 'Visa sponsorship available.' Check H-1B visa tracker data to prioritize.
5
Network with Indian ML Engineers in the US
Join Discord communities, Reddit (r/MachineLearning), and LinkedIn groups focused on India-to-US transitions. Get referrals from people who made the jump—referrals bypass many filtering steps and increase visa sponsorship odds by 3-4x.
6
Prepare for Behavioral Interviews with US Context
US companies ask: 'Tell me about a time you shipped a high-impact project.' Adapt Indian experience to US expectations: emphasize ownership, cross-functional collaboration, and business impact. Practice STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) with specific metrics.
FAQ

India to US Machine Learning Engineer — common questions.

Do I need to move to the US before applying for jobs?
No. You can apply while in India, but you must be willing to relocate and authorized to work (or sponsored). Some companies interview remotely; others require you to be in the US within 30-60 days of hire. Be explicit about relocation willingness in your applications.
What's the timeline from application to visa approval?
Typically 4-6 months: 1-2 months for interviews, 1-2 months for offer negotiation and visa sponsorship paperwork, 2-3 months for H-1B processing (if lottery-eligible). Start early; many companies have visa quotas and fill them quickly.
Is an MS degree in the US required?
No, but it helps. Many Indian engineers land US jobs without a US degree. What matters: proven technical depth, a strong portfolio, system design skills, and applying to companies that actively sponsor. An MS removes visa uncertainty but isn't mandatory.
How much should I study for US ML interviews?
Plan 3-4 months of dedicated prep: 4-6 weeks on coding (LeetCode Medium/Hard), 4-6 weeks on system design, 2-3 weeks on behavioral prep, and 2-3 weeks of mock interviews. US interviews are deeper than typical Indian interviews; don't underestimate prep time.
Will my Indian experience be valued?
Yes, if framed correctly. US companies value outcomes, not pedigree. Indian companies like Flipkart, Amazon India, and Ola are respected; emphasize the scale, impact, and technical depth of your work. Avoid vague descriptions; quantify everything.
What's the salary negotiation strategy?
US salaries for ML engineers are 3-5x India salaries. Research levels.fyi and Blind for your role/company/level. Don't anchor to Indian salary. Negotiate total comp (base + equity + bonus), not just base. First offer is often 15-20% below market; push back with data.
Should I apply as senior or mid-level?
Apply at the level matching your experience, not your Indian title. An Indian 'Senior Engineer' often maps to US 'Mid-level.' Visa sponsorship is easier for entry and mid-level roles (companies have more visas). Don't oversell; US interviewers can quickly assess your actual level.
