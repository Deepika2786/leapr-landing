Action Plan

Steps to land a US Technical Program Manager role from India.

1
1. Audit Your TPM Experience Against US Standards
Map your current role to US TPM competencies: roadmap ownership, cross-functional leadership, metrics-driven decision-making, and risk management. If you're in a PM, delivery, or technical lead role in India, identify specific TPM work you've done (not operations). Document 2-3 measurable outcomes: 'Led cross-team initiative reducing deployment time by 40%' or 'Defined quarterly roadmap for 3 engineering teams.'
2
2. Close Skill Gaps With Targeted Certifications & Portfolio Work
US employers expect TPMs to know agile frameworks, roadmap tools (Jira, Asana, Productboard), and analytics. Complete Google's Technical Program Manager Certificate or similar (4-6 weeks). Build a public portfolio: write 2-3 case studies on Medium or your blog showcasing how you managed cross-functional programs. Include data, trade-offs, and outcomes.
3
3. Strengthen Your US Network Before Applying
Referrals convert at 10x higher rates than cold applications. Join online communities: Reforge (Technical Program Management course), Product School, or LinkedIn groups for TPMs. Attend virtual meetups, webinars, and conferences (mind the time difference). Follow hiring managers and recruiters from target companies; engage thoughtfully with their posts. Aim for 5-10 warm connections before submitting applications.
4
4. Target Companies That Actively Sponsor Visas
Apply to FAANG (Amazon, Apple, Google, Meta, Microsoft), growth-stage startups (Stripe, Databricks, Scale AI), and consultancies (Deloitte, McKinsey digital). Check Glassdoor, Blind, and LinkedIn for visa sponsorship mentions. Avoid small companies unless they have a dedicated immigration budget. Use LinkedIn filters to search 'H-1B sponsorship' or check company career pages explicitly.
5
5. Craft a Resume Built for US Hiring Gatekeepers
Use US resume format: one page, no photo, no caste/religion/age. Lead with achievements, not responsibilities. Use metrics: 'Coordinated $2M project delivery across 4 teams' or 'Improved on-time delivery from 65% to 92%.' Highlight leadership of distributed teams and cross-functional influence. Include keywords: 'roadmap,' 'stakeholder management,' 'technical complexity,' 'agile/scrum.'
6
6. Prepare for US Technical Program Manager Interview Loops
Expect 4-5 rounds: recruiter screen (role fit, visa sponsorship), hiring manager (TPM experience, problem-solving), technical round (understand engineering trade-offs), cross-functional round (stakeholder collaboration), and sometimes a case study (project planning). Practice with peers or mentors. Use STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for behavioral questions. Be ready to explain India-to-US transition and visa timeline.
7
7. Navigate Visa & Relocation Logistics Early
During interviews, confirm the company sponsors visas and the estimated timeline (6-12 months for H-1B). Ask about relocation assistance, visa costs, and whether they file during premium processing. Many companies require you to start remotely from India while visa processes. Budget $2K-$5K for visa application, legal fees, and initial relocation.
FAQ

India to US Technical Program Manager — common questions.

Do I need a specific degree to become a TPM in the US?
No, but a bachelor's degree (any field) is standard. Most US TPM roles don't require an MBA, though it can help. Indian degrees are recognized if accredited by AICTE or equivalent. Many TPMs come from engineering, product, or operations backgrounds. Focus on demonstrating TPM skills and outcomes over credentials.
How long does the visa sponsorship process take?
H-1B sponsorship typically takes 6-12 months from offer to visa approval. Premium processing can reduce this to 3-4 months but costs an additional $2,500. You may start work remotely from India while the visa processes. Some companies require a start date only after visa approval; clarify this during negotiation.
What salary should I target as an Indian TPM moving to the US?
Entry-level TPM (L3/TPM1): $180K-$220K. Mid-level (L4/TPM2-3): $240K-$320K. Senior (L5/TPM4): $320K+. Total comp includes base salary (50-60%), stock vesting over 4 years, and annual bonus (10-25%). Salaries vary by cost of living: San Francisco and New York pay 20-30% more than other cities. Negotiate confidently; US companies budget for sponsorship costs.
Can I switch from a non-TPM role to TPM while in India?
Yes, but it's harder. If you're in PM, Engineering, or Delivery roles, start taking on TPM-adjacent work: leading cross-functional initiatives, owning roadmaps, mentoring junior PMs. Document these. Then apply to TPM roles in the US. Many TPMs transition from adjacent roles; frame your experience as foundational, not a career change.
Which US companies hire the most TPMs from India?
Amazon (TPM is core role), Google, Microsoft, Meta, and Apple actively hire from India. Growth startups like Stripe, Figma, and Notion sponsor visas frequently. Consulting firms (McKinsey, Deloitte, Accenture) hire digitally-focused TPMs. Enterprise software (Salesforce, Adobe, Workday) is also open. Check Blind and Glassdoor for current visa sponsorship feedback.
Do I need to be in the US to interview for a TPM role?
No. Most US companies conduct fully remote interviews for visa candidates. Time zone differences (India is 9.5-13.5 hours ahead) may require flexibility on meeting times, but companies usually accommodate. Only after offer, during visa processing, might they require you to be in the US.
How do I explain my India experience to US recruiters?
Be direct and proud of your experience. Frame it in US-relevant terms: 'Led 50-person engineering org' (not 'managed 50 reports'), 'shipped products used by 10M+ users,' 'improved system reliability from 98% to 99.9%.' Highlight distributed team management, complexity, and scale. US companies value global experience; position India work as proof of adaptability and impact, not lesser experience.
