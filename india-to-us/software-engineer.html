Action Plan

Steps to land a US Software Engineer role from India.

1
Assess Your Visa Eligibility Early
Determine which visa category fits your profile: H-1B (most common for software engineers, requires employer sponsorship and lottery), O-1 (extraordinary ability, rare), or EB-3 (employment-based green card, requires employer commitment). Discuss visa strategy with potential employers before accepting offers—some companies have unlimited H-1B numbers (cap-exempt), making sponsorship faster.
2
Rebuild Your Resume for US Standards
Convert multi-page Indian resume to concise one-pager. Lead with quantified achievements: 'Reduced API response time by 40%' instead of 'worked on backend optimization.' Highlight technologies that match US job descriptions (cloud platforms, containerization, modern frameworks). Remove photo, personal details, and academic honors. Include GitHub profile and portfolio links.
3
Master US-Style Technical Interviews
US coding interviews emphasize LeetCode-style problems, system design (how would you build Twitter?), and coding under time pressure. Practice on LeetCode Hard problems, study scalability concepts, and prepare 2-3 system design explanations. Mock interview platforms like Pramp or Interviewing.io with US-based engineers. Behavioral interviews use STAR format—prepare specific stories demonstrating impact.
4
Target Companies with Visa Sponsorship Track Records
Apply to FAANG companies, unicorns (Stripe, Figma), and large enterprises—they process hundreds of H-1B applications annually. Research using H1B visa databases (myvisajobs.com, h1bgrader.com) to see which companies sponsor roles matching yours. Avoid early-stage startups unless they commit to visa sponsorship. Mention visa sponsorship capability in cover letter early.
5
Prepare for Salary and Relocation Negotiations
Research US market rates on Levels.fyi and Blind (adjust for location and company). Expect 2-4x your current Indian salary. Negotiate total compensation including sign-on bonus ($20K-$100K+), relocation assistance ($10K-$30K), and stock options. Ask about remote-first roles or visa sponsorship timeline clarification. Don't accept an offer until visa sponsorship terms are in writing.
FAQ

India to US Software Engineer — common questions.

Do I need a US degree to get a Software Engineer job in the US from India?
No. US employers focus on skills and experience, not where you studied. However, a US degree or bootcamp makes visa sponsorship easier (some visa categories favor US education). Your strongest asset is demonstrable experience—real projects, contributions to open-source, and technical depth. A relevant degree (Computer Science, Engineering) from any country helps with initial screening.
What's the timeline from application to starting work in the US?
Plan 4-8 months: 1-2 months applications and interviews, 1 month offer negotiation and background check, 2-6 months visa processing (H-1B lottery happens in March, approval by October). Some companies sponsor faster visas (O-1, L-1) reducing timeline to 2-3 months. Start applying 6-12 months before your target start date.
Should I apply to US companies while still employed in India?
Yes, most Indian companies allow employees to interview for overseas roles. Be discreet and don't mention it to your current employer until you have an offer. US visa sponsorship requires you to be employed by the US company, so you can't start job-hunting as a freelancer or unemployed. Resign only after your offer is finalized and background check clears.
Is H-1B the only visa option for Software Engineers moving from India to the US?
H-1B is the most common (specialty occupation visa), but alternatives exist: O-1 for exceptional talent (rare for engineers), L-1 if transferring within a company, and EB-3 employment-based green card (longer process, requires labor certification). F-1 student visa works if pursuing a US master's degree. H-1B has annual caps and lottery, so don't rely on it exclusively—explore other routes with employers.
How do I overcome timezone and remote work challenges?
Many US tech companies now offer remote roles or flexible schedules. During interviews, clarify expectations—ask if the role is remote-first or requires relocation. If remote, you can stay in India initially and relocate later. Some companies sponsor relocation after 6-12 months. Discuss timezone overlap during salary negotiation (working early mornings or evenings aligns with US business hours).
What salary should I expect moving from India to the US?
US Software Engineer salaries range $120K-$300K+ depending on location, company, and level. San Francisco/NYC pay 20-30% more than other US cities. Check Levels.fyi for your target company and role level. Mid-level engineers from India typically land $130K-$200K total compensation (base + stock + bonus) in their first US role. Negotiate aggressively—companies expect it.
